<p><p>The casting superalloy market comprises three primary types: nickel-based, iron-based, and cobalt-based superalloys. Nickel-based cast superalloys, known for their high-temperature strength and corrosion resistance, dominate applications in aerospace and power generation. Iron-based superalloys, typically more affordable, are utilized in automotive and industrial applications due to their good mechanical properties. Cobalt-based superalloys offer exceptional wear resistance and are often used in medical implants and gas turbine components, catering to specialized high-performance markets.</p></p>
