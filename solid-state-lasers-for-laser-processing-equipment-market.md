<p><p>Solid-state lasers for laser processing equipment encompass several types, primarily picosecond and nanosecond lasers. Picosecond solid-state lasers emit pulses on the order of trillionths of a second, offering high precision and minimal thermal damage, making them ideal for micro-machining and delicate material processing. In contrast, nanosecond solid-state lasers produce longer pulse durations (billionths of a second), suitable for applications like cutting, welding, and surface treatment, where a balance between energy delivery and thermal impact is crucial. Both types enhance efficiency in industrial applications.</p></p>
