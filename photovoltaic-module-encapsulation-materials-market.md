<p><p>The photovoltaic module encapsulation materials market comprises three primary types: EVA film, PVB film, and TPU film. EVA (ethylene vinyl acetate) film is the most widely used for encapsulating solar cells due to its excellent transparency, adhesion, and durability. PVB (polyvinyl butyral) film offers superior moisture resistance and is often used in high-performance applications. TPU (thermoplastic polyurethane) film, known for its flexibility and mechanical strength, is emerging as a viable alternative, providing better temperature stability and environmental resistance.</p></p>
