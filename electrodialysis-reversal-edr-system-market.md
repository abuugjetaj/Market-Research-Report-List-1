<p><p>Electrodialysis Reversal (EDR) is an innovative water treatment technology that employs an electric field to drive ions through selective ion-exchange membranes, effectively separating salts from water. This system is particularly advantageous in desalination processes, wastewater treatment, and water recovery, enabling the production of high-quality freshwater from saline sources. EDR operates by periodically reversing the polarity of the electrodes, thereby minimizing membrane scaling and fouling, which enhances operational efficiency.</p><p>The EDR system market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for sustainable water solutions amid global water scarcity. Advancements in membrane materials and technology are further propelling market expansion, as they enhance the system's efficiency and operational lifespan. <p><p>The Electrodialysis Reversal (EDR) system market comprises two primary types: Continuous Electrodialysis and Batch Electrodialysis. Continuous Electrodialysis operates in a steady flow mode, allowing for uninterrupted processing of solutions, making it ideal for large-scale applications like desalination and wastewater treatment. In contrast, Batch Electrodialysis processes solutions in discrete volumes, often suited for smaller-scale operations or specialized applications. Both systems utilize electric potential to drive ion migration through selective membranes, effectively separating ions and purifying solutions.</p></p>
